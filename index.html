Olivia Spargur's Guide: How To Prepare for an Interview

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make prepare for the most successful interview of your career!

Preparation time: 30 minutes
Cool rating: 50% more cool

Have you ever gone to an interiew shaking in your boots because you have no idea how to answer their grilling questions? Do you just have stage fright when it comes to being put under the career spotlight? Then look no further than these super easy, super relaxed tips that will guarantee a higher success rate of landing that job!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Internet Access
  • Notepad
  • Pencil/Pen
  • Clock
  • Business Casual Outfit
  • Mirror (optional) or quiet room

  1. Do some research about the company ahead of time.

    It's always helpful to know the company's mission, purpose, etc. Gather as much information as you can to understand what company you are trying to get into.

  2. Anticipate and practice your answers to common interview questions.

    With your research, also look up common interview questions. Write out how you would answer each question and rehearse a little, so you have a basis to go off of.

    A panel of interviewers can be intimidating, but remember to breathe and take your time answering. Be honest to yourself.

  3. Come up with a good strength and weakness.

    This may be a question that pops up during the interview. Having 2-3 responses is great. Highlight some of your better qualities. A weakness example may be: "I am not the best idea generator, but I am great at building upon ideas."

  4. Dress the part.

    Appearance can make or break your interview. Come clean and presentable. Business casual attire is always a safe go-to, but you may also opt for a more formal or casual option. As long as it is appropriate and shows how professional you can be.

  5. Get there on time.

    Showing up 10-15 minutes early is a great look! Your interview starts before you enter that room. Show responsibility.

  6. Sit up straight and pay attention.

    Appearance is crucial. Posture represents professionality. Good posture helps you focus better. Think before you speak. You don't need to fill every moment of silence. Focus on listening rather than prepping responses.

  7. Be yourself

    Avoid cliches. Just relax. They are hiring a human, not a robot. Don't strive for perfection. Strive for human.
